CARD 1

Atestia vs. ValidMind

THEIR POSITIONING

"Agentic AI scales exponentially. Your governance should too."

Workflow-and-document governance platform for AI agents, models, and applications. Series A ($11M). Magenta brand. Sales-led pricing.

OUR POSITIONING

"The runtime evidence layer for regulated AI."

Runtime MCP capture + open standard governed by industry working group + commercial enterprise tier. Same buyer (MRM), different architecture.

3 STRENGTHS — them

  • 1. Broader AI agent coverage (autonomous agents + ML models + LLM apps in one platform)
  • 2. Risk-framework integration with pre-built tier routing (SR 26-2 + EU AI Act + OSFI E-23)
  • 3. Existing General Bank of Canada deployment with 70% validation time reduction case study

3 WEAKNESSES — them

  • 1. Workflow/document-centric, not runtime-capture. Cannot answer "show me the invocation log" with a single query.
  • 2. Proprietary spec. No industry working group. Vendor risk committee scrutiny.
  • 3. No OpenLineage adapter. No MCP-native architecture. Sits adjacent to where vendor models actually invoke.

"WE'RE ALREADY EVALUATING VALIDMIND" — 3 RESPONSES

  1. 1. "Great. Their workflow side is strong. Are you also evaluating runtime capture? Because the SR 26-2 examiner question is 'what happened in production six months ago' — and that's the runtime side, not the workflow side."
  2. 2. "Do you know if your vendor risk committee will accept a proprietary attestation format? Tier-4 is governed by a 501(c)(6) working group. ValidMind owns their format. That difference matters at procurement time."
  3. 3. "We're complementary, not competitive. Many of our pilots run alongside ValidMind. Tier-4 captures the runtime; ValidMind manages the workflow. Want to see how the integration handoff works?"

CARD 2

Atestia vs. Yields.io

THEIR POSITIONING

"Manage AI and Model risk in one place."

Enterprise MRM platform. 50+ named bank customers (HSBC, BNP Paribas, Euroclear). EU heritage. Sales-led pricing. Document/approval workflow architecture.

OUR ANGLE

"Tier-4 captures the runtime; Yields manages the workflow."

Yields owns the back half of MRM (approval, validation workflow, sign-off). Atestia owns the front half (runtime capture, packet generation). Co-deployable.

3 STRENGTHS — them

  • 1. 50+ enterprise bank customers including HSBC and BNP Paribas. Massive credibility moat.
  • 2. Mature MRM workflow features: approval routing, sign-off, audit trails, validation evidence management.
  • 3. EU regulatory expertise (especially relevant for European banks but also growing US presence).

3 WEAKNESSES — them

  • 1. Architectural assumption is approval workflow + documentation. They have no runtime invocation capture for vendor models. Cannot natively answer SR 26-2 invocation-level questions.
  • 2. Proprietary attestation formats. No industry working group governance.
  • 3. SaaS workflow platform, not MCP-native. Cannot drop into a Bedrock AgentCore configuration in 60 seconds.

"WE'RE EVALUATING YIELDS.IO" — 3 RESPONSES

  1. 1. "Yields is excellent for the MRM workflow layer — approval routing, validation evidence storage, audit trails. We're the layer before that. Our runtime capture feeds your Yields workflow."
  2. 2. "What's your timeline for going to your Fed/OCC examiner with vendor model evidence? Yields doesn't capture invocation-level lineage at runtime. Atestia does. Different parts of the problem."
  3. 3. "Yields has incredible customer logos. We can name them too once we're in production. The question is which architecture closes the SR 26-2 invocation-level evidence gap. Want to see a packet from a real lineage trail vs. a workflow document?"

KILLER QUESTION

"Can Yields produce an OCC 2013-29 packet directly from a vendor model invocation log, or does it require manually-attached validation documents that your team uploads to their workflow?"

CARD 4

Atestia vs. Build-in-house

THEIR POSITIONING

"Our Model Risk Office is staffing up. We can build this."

Capital One, JPMorgan publicly hiring "Senior Manager, Data Science, Model Risk Office" with LangChain + Vector DB + LLMOps scope. KPMG, PwC steer bank clients toward internal capability builds.

OUR ANGLE

"Build the things only you can build. Buy the things every bank needs."

Vendor model attestation packet format is industry-standard, not bank-differentiating. The bank-differentiated work is upstream (model selection, validation methodology, business policy).

WHY BUILD-IN-HOUSE FAILS HERE

  • 1. Time-to-value. Internal build = 9-18 months. SR 26-2 exam cycle is 2027. Atestia install = 60 seconds. Customer KMS integration = 4 weeks. First packet = 8 weeks.
  • 2. Format ratification. Examiners want standard formats. A bank-proprietary packet format requires examiner negotiation. Industry standard (Tier-4) is accepted by default.
  • 3. Maintenance cost. Spec evolves. Every regulatory update (CCAR, DFAST, future EU AI Act, NAIC AI Bulletin) requires bank-side engineering. Atestia ships the updates as part of the platform.
  • 4. Auditor reception. Big-4 audit firms working with bank clients will start accepting Tier-4-Conformant packets as ingestion format. A proprietary format requires per-bank audit explanation.
  • 5. Risk concentration. A 3-person MRM tech team building runtime capture is single-point-of-failure. Atestia is a managed platform with SLAs.

KILLER QUESTION

"Your in-house MRM platform team — are they delivering specific bank-differentiated capabilities, or are they reinventing examiner packet formats? Because every Tier-1 bank's in-house team is reinventing the same five packet formats simultaneously."

COMMON OBJECTION + RESPONSE

"We don't trust vendor lock-in." The spec is CC-BY-4.0 open. The reference implementation is MPL-2.0 open source. If Atestia disappears tomorrow, your runtime capture continues against the open-source server. The format is publicly documented. No lock-in.
