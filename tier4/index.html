Open Reference Architecture v1.1

The open standard for vendor model attestation in regulated AI.

Tier-4 is the open specification, reference implementation, and standards body for capturing vendor model lineage at the moment of invocation by US-bank agentic AI platforms. Published under CC-BY-4.0. The reference implementation is MPL-2.0.

Read the spec v1.1 View reference implementation

Why this exists

SR 26-2 was issued by the Federal Reserve, OCC, and FDIC on April 17, 2026. Footnote 3 (page 3) explicitly carves generative AI and agentic AI out of the guidance's scope, while the same paragraph requires that "a banking organization's risk management and governance practices should guide the determination of appropriate governance and controls for any tools, processes, or systems not covered." Section VII (pages 10-11) preserves the bank's accountability for understanding, validating, and monitoring every vendor model their agents invoke.

Across publicly disclosed bank agentic AI deployments, vendor models are invoked at rates of millions per day. Manual reconstruction of vendor evidence at examination time is not operationally feasible at that cardinality.

Tier-4 provides the technical specification for capturing this evidence at invocation time, in a format common across vendors and institutions, machine-readable for downstream tooling, and examiner-defensible for regulatory review — anchored on the regulatory citations that DO apply to the use case: SR 26-2 §VII (vendor model understanding), the 2023 Interagency TPRM Guidance, BCBS 239 Principles 1-11 (bank-facing), ECOA Reg B §1002.9 + CFPB Circular 2022-03 / 2023-03 (AI-driven adverse action), and EU AI Act Article 50 + Annex III(5)(b).

What's in v1.1

Section 4

Lineage record schema

JSON Schema 2020-12 canonical schema with required fields, PII handling, immutability rules, and field-stability guarantees.

Section 5

OCC 2013-29 packet format

47 fields organized into 7 sections. Materialized on-demand in under 15 seconds. JSON + PDF output.

Section 6

SR 26-2 attestation pack

5-artifact bundle: model card v1.0, OCC questionnaire, lineage trail sample, monitoring spec, SOC 2 control matrix.

Section 7

BCBS 239 aggregation reference

Mapping to the 11 bank-facing BCBS 239 principles (governance, data architecture, aggregation, reporting). Principles 12-14 govern supervisors and are out of scope for any vendor.

Section 8

Retention model

7-year retention horizon. Customer-held KMS keys. AES-256-GCM at rest. Migration rights preserved.

Section 9

Conformance levels

Three levels: Implementer (free, self-declared), Conformant (test-suite passing), Certified (annual third-party audit).

Get started

For developers

npm install @atestia/tier4-mcp-server
npx tier4-mcp-server

Then add to Claude Desktop, Bedrock AgentCore, OpenAI Assistants, or any MCP-compatible AI runtime. See the README for examples.

For bank MRM teams

Pull the spec at tier4.org/spec/v1.1 and walk it through your team. If you would like to be a founding charter member of the Working Group, see the Charter.

For vendor LLM providers

Conform your evidence emit format to §4 of the spec. If you ship a Tier-4-Conformant adapter for Bedrock, Anthropic, OpenAI, Azure, or Vertex, list it in the Conformant Implementations Registry.

Tier-4 Compliance Working Group

The Working Group is a Delaware 501(c)(6) industry association filing in process Q3 2026. Three membership tiers: Charter Member (founding 10), General Member ($25K/yr), Implementer Member (free, listed in registry).

Steering Committee of seven seats. The Atestia Inc. designee holds a permanent non-voting seat in recognition of founding-sponsor role. All spec ratification is one-vote-per-Member.
