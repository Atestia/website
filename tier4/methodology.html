Methodology v1.0

Tier-4 Bank MRM Readiness Index

A quarterly published score, 0-100, measuring US bank readiness for the SR 26-2 examination cycle on eight sub-dimensions. v0 (Q3 2026) published from public-record signals only. v1.0 (Q4 2026) introduces private-mode subscriber scoring with live platform-emitted evidence.

The 8 sub-dimensions

Dimension 1

Vendor model inventory coverage

Does the bank have a documented, current registry of every vendor model invoked by its agents?

Dimension 2

Agentic AI lineage substrate

Is there runtime evidence capture at vendor model invocation time, or only retrospective reconstruction?

Dimension 3

SR 26-2 packet generation capability

Can the bank produce an examination-ready packet within 30 seconds on demand?

Dimension 4

ECOA Reg B adverse-action defensibility

For agentic credit decisions, is the reasoning chain reconstructible and the Adverse Action Notice compliant?

Dimension 5

Banker prompt compliance controls

Is there real-time scanning of banker LLM prompts for PII / Reg FD / MNPI / fair-lending breaches?

Dimension 6

BCBS 239 column-level lineage

Does risk-data aggregation include column-level provenance for AI/ML model contributions?

Dimension 7

Cross-business-unit AI governance

Does the Chief AI Officer have visibility into vendor models across all business lines?

Dimension 8

MCP server attestation coverage

Has the bank certified the MCP servers its AI platforms plug into?

Scoring sources (v0 — public record only)

The v0 Index, published Q3 2026, scores banks on the basis of public-record signals only. No private data is used. The methodology is fully transparent and reproducible.

  • · 10-K model risk disclosures (Form 10-K Item 1A Risk Factors + supplementary MRM commentary)
  • · Public OCC, Federal Reserve, FDIC enforcement actions and consent decrees
  • · Public CFPB consumer complaint database
  • · MRM team size from public LinkedIn employment data
  • · Public AI announcements (Citi Arc, JPM Index AI, BNY Eliza, etc.) with vendor model counts where disclosed
  • · BCBS 239 compliance language in annual reports
  • · Public conference appearances by CRO, CCO, and Head of MRM

Private-mode scoring (v1.0 — subscribers)

v1.0 introduces a private-mode subscriber score that uses live evidence from the bank's Tier-4-conformant implementation. Private-mode scores incorporate runtime evidence freshness, packet generation latency, and conformance test suite passing levels.

Subscribed banks always score higher in private mode than they would in public mode, because private mode has richer data.

Distribution

v0 published Q3 2026 with American Banker as the founding research distribution partner. Subsequent quarterly editions distributed via the same channel.
