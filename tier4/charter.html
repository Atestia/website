Tier-4 Compliance Working Group

Delaware 501(c)(6) industry association. Filing in process Q3 2026. Founding sponsor: Atestia Inc.

Mission

The Tier-4 Compliance Working Group is organized to develop, maintain, and promote open specifications for the runtime governance of vendor models invoked by agentic artificial intelligence platforms at US-supervised financial institutions.

Specifically, the Group addresses the regulatory accountability gap created by SR 26-2 (effective April 17, 2026), which explicitly excludes generative and agentic artificial intelligence from definitional model scope while retaining institutional accountability for every vendor model the institution's agents invoke.

Membership tiers

Class I — Charter Member (founding 10)

  • · Annual dues: $25,000 (waived for the first 12 months)
  • · Steering Committee seat (three-year term)
  • · Voting weight: 3× on spec ratification votes
  • · Named in spec preamble in perpetuity
  • · Open application period: Q3 2026

Class II — General Member

  • · Annual dues: $25,000
  • · Voting weight: 1× on spec ratification votes
  • · 30-day embargo preview of spec working drafts before public release
  • · Private-mode access to the quarterly Tier-4 Bank MRM Readiness Index

Class III — Implementer Member

  • · No dues
  • · Listed in the public Conformant Implementations Registry
  • · May use the descriptor "Tier-4-Conformant" subject to the Trademark Policy

Eligibility

The following entity types may apply for Class I or II membership:

  • · US-supervised banks of any size
  • · Vendor LLM providers (Anthropic, OpenAI, AWS, Microsoft, Google, etc.)
  • · Big-Four audit firms (KPMG, Deloitte, EY, PwC) and other audit firms
  • · Insurance underwriters (D&O, E&O, cyber) and insurance brokers
  • · RegTech vendors providing complementary services to bank MRM teams
  • · Regulator-adjacent organizations (research, policy, NIST)

Banks that are direct customers of Atestia's commercial platform receive a 50% discount on Class II dues for the first contract year.

Governance

The Working Group is governed by a Steering Committee of seven seats: three Charter Member seats, two General Member seats (elected two-year terms), one Atestia Inc. designee seat (permanent, non-voting on spec ratification), and one regulator-adjacent observer seat (appointed two-year terms).

The Steering Committee elects a Chair annually from among its members. The Chair must be from a Class I or Class II member institution. The Atestia designee may not serve as Chair.
