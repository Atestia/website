USE CASE

SR 26-2 + ECOA Reg B

Credit decisioning + BCBS 239 lineage

The challenge

The bank had deployed an agentic credit decisioning system in late 2025. The system invoked vendor models from Experian, Equifax, and Plaid through a Claude-powered orchestrator. By Q1 2026 the system was processing thousands of decisions per week.

When SR 26-2 was issued in April 2026, the MRM team initiated a readiness review. They discovered that no runtime evidence existed for vendor model invocations. The orchestrator's logs captured request/response bodies but in a format unsuitable for examination packets. Producing an OCC 2013-29 questionnaire required ~3 weeks of manual reconstruction from logs, email threads, and vendor SOC 2 reports.

The deployment

The team installed the Atestia Tier-4 MCP server in a sandbox in week one. By the end of week two, they had instrumented the orchestrator to write a lineage record on every vendor model invocation. Week three covered MRM analyst UI rollout and packet generation testing. Week four was production cutover.

Atestia Solutions Engineering supported a 4-hour weekly standup throughout. The total engineering effort on the bank's side was approximately 80 hours.
