30 questions from bank MRM teams across the pilot lifecycle.

Pre-installation

Do we need to be on a specific cloud to use Atestia?

No. Atestia is cloud-agnostic. The reference implementation runs anywhere Node 20+ runs. The commercial Atestia Platform is hosted on AWS US-East-1 by default but supports Azure (Frankfurt + East US) and GCP (us-east-4) on request. For Tier-1 contracts we deploy into customer VPC.

What's the typical install time from contract signature to first lineage record?

4 to 8 weeks for production deployment. The open-source reference implementation installs in 60 seconds; first sandbox lineage record can be generated the same afternoon. Production cutover requires customer KMS integration (1-2 weeks), agent platform instrumentation (1-2 weeks), and InfoSec review.

Does Atestia work with our existing MRM platform (ValidMind, Yields, etc.)?

Yes. Atestia captures runtime evidence; existing MRM platforms manage approval workflow and validation evidence storage. Many deployments run Atestia alongside ValidMind or Yields. Atestia exports OpenLineage events and supports webhook forwarding to your existing platform.

What's the minimum vendor model count to justify deployment?

Approximately 30 vendor models if you have ongoing examination cycles. For banks with fewer than 30 models, the open-source tier may be sufficient. Atestia Platform Enterprise is most economical when you generate 8+ packets per year or have more than 100 vendor models.

Where does my customer data go?

It doesn't. Atestia captures SHA-256 hashes of vendor model inputs and structured metadata (vendor ID, latency, agent ID, decision context). Raw payloads never reach Atestia infrastructure. The architecture is intentionally PII-free.

Installation + configuration

Which AI agent platforms are officially supported?

AWS Bedrock AgentCore, Anthropic Claude Desktop, Cursor, OpenAI Assistants (via wrapper), LangChain (TypeScript and Python), and any MCP-conformant client. Integration guides for each are in github.com/Atestia/tier4-mcp-server/tree/main/examples.

How does the customer KMS integration work?

Atestia uses envelope encryption. Customer holds the master KMS key (AWS KMS, Azure Key Vault, or GCP Cloud KMS). Atestia generates per-record data keys, encrypts each lineage record at rest, and stores only the wrapped key. The customer can revoke key access; Atestia loses access immediately.

Can we self-host Atestia in our own VPC?

Yes, for Tier-1 enterprise contracts. We provide a Terraform module and infrastructure-as-code templates for AWS, Azure, and GCP. Customer operates the infrastructure; Atestia operates the application layer with SaaS-style updates.

What identity providers do you support?

SAML 2.0 and SCIM for Enterprise tier. Tested with Okta, Auth0, Microsoft Entra ID, Ping Identity, and Google Workspace. Clerk-backed authentication for Atestia Pro Starter/Pro/Scale tiers. Multi-factor authentication is required for administrative actions across all tiers.

How do we configure retention policies?

Default is 7 years SR 26-2-compliant. Customers can configure shorter retention via policy (subject to record-keeping obligations). All retention changes are logged. Cold storage tier (S3 + KMS) activates automatically after 90 days; customer KMS access required for retrieval.

First packet generation

How long does a packet take to generate?

OCC 2013-29 packet: 30-60 seconds API time, 4 hours wall-clock with MRM analyst review (formatting, footnotes, sign-off). SR 26-2 attestation pack: 2-3 minutes API time, full pack delivered in 24 hours including analyst review. BCBS 239 aggregation reference: 1-2 minutes for typical CCAR Y-14A scope.

Can we customize packet templates for our bank?

Yes. The Tier-4 spec defines the canonical structure; bank-specific footnotes, signatures, and supplementary attachments are configurable. Custom packet generators (e.g., bank-specific CCAR templates) are supported via the pluggable PacketGenerator interface.

What if our examiner asks for a packet format we haven't tested?

Our Solutions Engineering team builds custom packet generators on a 7-business-day turnaround for Enterprise customers. The cost is included in your subscription for the first 2 custom formats per year; additional formats are billed at $25K-$100K per format depending on complexity.

Can we run the packet generators against historical lineage?

Yes, against any lineage captured by Atestia. For pre-Atestia historical data, we offer a backfill service that ingests CloudWatch / Snowflake / custom log sources into Atestia-conformant lineage records. Backfill is billed as professional services ($150K-$500K typical scope).

How do we share packets with our examiner?

Packets are delivered as JSON or PDF. Atestia provides a secure share link with expiration controls and audit logs (who downloaded when). For sensitive examinations, packets can be exported with watermark and revocable access controls.

Production scale

What's the throughput ceiling?

Targeted at GA (Q4 2026): single-tenant Atestia Platform Enterprise sustaining 10,000 lineage records per second with 100,000-record burst; Atestia Pro multi-tenant at 1,000 records per second per tenant. These are the design targets validated against synthetic load patterns sized to top-quartile bank agent invocation volumes. Production validation against a real customer tenant follows first paid pilot.

What's the latency added to vendor model calls?

p50: ~5ms. p95: ~25ms. p99: ~100ms. Capture is asynchronous to vendor model invocation by default — Atestia does not block the agent. Synchronous mode (block until captured) adds 20-30ms p95 if your bank policy requires guaranteed capture before agent action.

What's the SLA?

Target SLA at GA (Q4 2026): 99.9% monthly uptime for Atestia Platform Enterprise, RPO 15 minutes, RTO 1 hour, with service credits at 5% of monthly fees per 0.1% below target. Tier-1 contracts will target 99.95% with higher credit multipliers. Production SLA commitments are written into the Enterprise MSA at contract execution; until production GA, this site does not publish live SLA telemetry.

How is high availability handled?

Multi-AZ in primary region, with cross-region replication on customer request for disaster recovery. RDS Multi-AZ for hot storage. S3 cross-region replication for cold storage. Stateless API tier with auto-scaling.

Can we monitor Atestia from our existing observability stack?

Atestia emits OpenTelemetry-compliant metrics, structured logs (JSON), and audit events. OTLP-compatible observability vendors (Datadog, Splunk, Sumo Logic, Sentry, New Relic, Grafana Cloud) can ingest the stream. Customer-side dashboards and alerts are configured via the standard provider integration patterns; we publish reference dashboards alongside Platform GA.

Examiner readiness

Has the OCC accepted a Tier-4 packet at an examination?

The format is brand new (June 2026). No examination cycle has tested the format yet. However, the format mirrors the structure of existing OCC 2013-29 and Federal Reserve SR 11-7 evidence patterns that examiners have used for 12+ years. The Working Group is engaging examiners directly during the v1.2 RFC period to capture preferences before the first 2027 cycle.

What if our examiner wants something Atestia doesn't produce?

We modify the spec to match examiner workflow, not the other way around. Custom packet generators are built within 7 business days. The Tier-4 v1.1 RFC (v1.2 next) process is specifically designed to incorporate examiner feedback. If your examination experience reveals a format gap, we ship a new generator.

Is the Tier-4 spec accepted by Big-4 audit firms?

No firm has yet signed any partnership with Atestia or formally accepted the spec. The Working Group's Class II Member application opens publicly once the Delaware 501(c)(6) filing closes (target Q3 2026). The Tier-4 Approved Auditor program targets a Q1 2027 opening for firms to certify implementations. We will list any participating audit firm publicly once an LOI is signed.

Does Atestia replace our SR 11-7 / SR 26-2 validation process?

No. Atestia captures runtime evidence and generates packets. Pre-deployment validation work (model card creation, fairness testing, business policy alignment) remains your MRM team's responsibility. Atestia automates the evidence layer; validation remains intellectual labor.

Can we use the open-source tier for examiner-facing packets?

Yes, the open-source reference implementation generates fully Tier-4-conformant packets. However, the enterprise tier adds 7-year SR 26-2-compliant retention enforcement, customer-held KMS, indemnification, 24-7 support, and dedicated SE support during examination preparation. Most banks adopt the open-source tier in pilots and upgrade to enterprise pre-production.

Renewal + expansion

How is renewal pricing structured?

Year 1 ACV is locked. Year 2 includes a 5% uplift (capped at CPI + 2%); Year 3 includes 3% uplift. Multi-year discounts: 5% off Year 2, 8% off Year 3 with 3-year commitment at contract signing. Volume discounts available for banks generating 40+ packets per year.

What expansion paths are typical?

First-year scope is usually one business unit (e.g., commercial credit decisioning). Year 2 expansion: add adjacent units (consumer credit, AML, KYC). Year 3 expansion: add BCBS 239 lineage for risk data aggregation, then add ECOA Reg B Adverse Action automation. Cross-industry expansion (capital markets, insurance, healthcare) becomes available Q1-Q3 2027.

Can we negotiate custom packet generators into the contract?

Yes. Custom packet generators are negotiated as separate SOWs or as part of the Master Services Agreement. Typical scope: 2 custom formats per year included in Year 1, additional formats at $25K-$100K per format.

What happens if Atestia is acquired?

The Tier-4 specification will be governed by the Tier-4 Compliance Working Group, a Delaware 501(c)(6) industry association (filing in process Q3 2026), and is licensed CC-BY-4.0. The reference implementation is MPL-2.0; an acquisition does not affect a customer's ability to operate against the open-source code. Enterprise customer contracts at execution include change-of-control provisions ensuring service continuity through the contract term — the specific continuity language is in the Enterprise MSA template available on request.

Can we become a Working Group Charter Member?

Yes. First 10 institutions to apply as Class I Charter Members receive Steering Committee seat, 3× voting weight on spec ratification, first-12-month dues waiver, and named-in-perpetuity placement in spec preamble. Apply at tier4/charter.
