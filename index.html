Open Reference Architecture v1.1

The runtime evidence layer for SR 26-2 at US banks.

Tier-4 is the open specification, reference implementation, and standards body for capturing vendor model lineage at the moment of invocation by US-bank agentic AI platforms. It is published under CC-BY-4.0. The reference implementation is MPL-2.0.

Read the spec v1.1 View reference implementation

Why this exists

SR 26-2 was issued by the Federal Reserve, OCC, and FDIC on April 17, 2026. Paragraph II.B explicitly carves out generative AI and agentic AI from definitional model scope. Paragraph III.A holds institutions accountable for every vendor model their agents call under "existing model risk management principles."

The first SR 26-2 examination cycle is Q1 2027. At Citi Arc, JPMorgan Chase Index AI, Goldman Athena Commercial, and similar agentic banking platforms, vendor models are invoked at rates of millions per day. Manual reconstruction of vendor evidence at examination time is not operationally feasible at that cardinality.

Tier-4 provides the technical specification for capturing this evidence at invocation time, in a format common across vendors and institutions, machine-readable for downstream tooling, and examiner-defensible for regulatory review.

Comply Inc. shipped the broker-dealer analog (ComplyAI MCP Server, April 22, 2026) for SEC and FINRA regulated institutions. Tier-4 is the bank-side analog for OCC, Federal Reserve, and FDIC supervised institutions. The two are complementary, not competitive.

What's in v1.1

Section 4

Lineage record schema

JSON Schema 2020-12 canonical schema with required fields, PII handling, immutability rules, and field-stability guarantees.

Section 5

OCC 2013-29 packet format

47 fields organized into 7 sections. Materialized on-demand in under 15 seconds. JSON + PDF output.

Section 6

SR 26-2 attestation pack

5-artifact bundle: model card v1.0, OCC questionnaire, lineage trail sample, monitoring spec, SOC 2 control matrix.

Section 7

BCBS 239 aggregation reference

Explicit mapping to all 14 BCBS 239 principles across the four sections (governance, aggregation, reporting, supervisory review).

Section 8

Retention model

7-year retention horizon. Customer-held KMS keys. AES-256-GCM at rest. Migration rights preserved.

Section 9

Conformance levels

Three levels: Implementer (free, self-declared), Conformant (test-suite passing), Certified (annual third-party audit).

Get started

For developers

npm install @atestia/tier4-mcp-server
npx tier4-mcp-server

Then add to Claude Desktop, Bedrock AgentCore, OpenAI Assistants, or any MCP-compatible AI runtime. See the README for examples.

For bank MRM teams

Pull the spec at tier4.org/spec/v1.1 and walk it through your team. If you'd like to be a founding charter member of the Working Group, see the Charter.

For vendor LLM providers

Conform your evidence emit format to §4 of the spec. If you ship a Tier-4-Conformant adapter for Bedrock, Anthropic, OpenAI, Azure, or Vertex, list it in the Conformant Implementations Registry.

Tier-4 Compliance Working Group

The Working Group is a Delaware 501(c)(6) industry association filing in process Q3 2026. Three membership tiers: Charter Member (founding 10), General Member ($25K/yr), Implementer Member (free, listed in registry).

Steering Committee of seven seats. The Atestia Inc. designee holds a permanent non-voting seat in recognition of founding-sponsor role. All spec ratification is one-vote-per-Member.
