Service Level Agreement (SLA)

Document version 1.0 · Effective on Atestia Platform Enterprise GA (target Q4 2026)

Pre-GA status. Atestia Platform Enterprise is in development as of June 2026. The SLA targets below are commitments that will be effective at Platform Enterprise GA. Until GA, this document represents Atestia's published service-level intent; specific commitments are written into the Enterprise Master Services Agreement (MSA) at contract execution.

1. Service definition

"Service" means the production hosted Atestia Platform Enterprise multi-tenant or single-tenant API, accessible via the production endpoints documented in the Enterprise MSA. Self-serve tiers (Free, Starter, Pro, Scale) are governed by the Terms of Service and are not subject to this SLA.

3. Severity definitions

  • Severity 1 (Critical). Production service unavailable; complete loss of Customer Data access; security incident materially affecting Customer Data.
  • Severity 2 (High). Production service materially degraded; non-critical features unavailable; performance below SLA but service available.
  • Severity 3 (Medium). Minor functional issue with workaround available; non-blocking bug.
  • Severity 4 (Low). Cosmetic issue; documentation gap; feature request.

5. Service credits

For each calendar month in which Atestia fails to meet the uptime target for an Enterprise tier, the customer is entitled to service credits per the following schedule:

Uptime achievedCredit (% of monthly fees)
≥ target uptime0%

target − 0.5% to target − 0.1%15%
target − 1.0% to target − 0.5%30%
below target − 1.0%50% (maximum)

Maximum total monthly credit: 50% of monthly fees. Credits are applied to the customer's next invoice and require a written claim within 30 days of the affected month-end.

6. SLA exclusions

Downtime caused by the following is excluded from uptime calculations:

  • Scheduled maintenance windows (announced ≥ 5 business days in advance).
  • Force majeure events.
  • Customer-side actions (e.g., customer revoking KMS keys, customer-side credential issues).
  • Issues with sub-processors operating within their own SLAs but causing dependent service interruption (we work to coordinate but are not financially liable beyond pass-through credit).
  • Beta features explicitly labeled "Beta" or "Preview".

7. Termination for chronic breach

If Atestia fails to meet uptime targets for 3 consecutive months or any 4 months within a 12-month period, the customer may terminate the Enterprise MSA for material breach with 30-day notice and receive a pro-rated refund of prepaid fees for the unused portion of the contract term.

8. Incident communication

During Sev 1 and Sev 2 incidents, Atestia provides hourly status updates to the customer's designated incident contact. Post-incident, Atestia publishes a Root Cause Analysis (RCA) within 5 business days for Sev 1 incidents and within 10 business days for Sev 2 incidents.

9. Relationship to other documents

This SLA is incorporated into the Enterprise Master Services Agreement at contract execution. In case of conflict, the MSA controls. The canonical sub-processor list, Privacy Policy, and Terms of Service are referenced rather than restated.
